New Hampshire General Election: Know Where Your Candidate Stands on Union Issues

By JP Dustin | September 15, 2024

With the New Hampshire state primary election concluded, voters are now preparing to cast their ballots in the general election on November 5, 2024. It is more important than ever to understand where your candidates stand on key issues, particularly their positions on union rights and labor protections.

In New Hampshire, where "right-to-work" legislation has been a recurring debate, it is crucial for voters who care about the strength of their unions to stay informed about each candidateâ€™s stance on labor rights. New Hampshire has seen multiple attempts to pass "right-to-work" laws, which would prevent unions from requiring workers to pay union dues as a condition of employment. This is part of a larger national push that many see as an effort to weaken organized labor. In fact, states that have passed "right-to-work" laws often see lower wages and reduced benefits for workers.

Will your candidate support collective bargaining, fair wages, and worker protections? These key issues are critical for preserving workers' rights in New Hampshire, a state where union density has helped secure better working conditions for thousands. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, union members in New Hampshire earn on average 11% more than their non-union counterparts, underscoring the importance of protecting the right to organize.

Follow this link to see how your candidate voted on the postponement of the most recent "right-to-work" bill in NH. HB1377 died in chamber when members of the House voted to indefinitely postpone taking up the bill for the session. The bill was killed in a 212-168 vote, largely along party lines, with many arguing that it would have undermined collective bargaining and weakened unions across the state.

This is just the most recent in a long series of attempts to pass such a state law. Similar bills have been proposed and defeated several times in the past decade, each time drawing sharp lines between labor advocates and those supporting the "right-to-work" agenda. New Hampshire remains one of the few states in the region without a "right-to-work" law, a status that labor unions have fought hard to maintain.

Your vote in the general election on November 5, 2024, will determine the policies that directly affect unions and working people in New Hampshire. Make sure your voice is heard in this crucial election.
