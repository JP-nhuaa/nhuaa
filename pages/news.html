Local 633 Officially Nominates Candidates for Upcoming Union Election

By JP Dustin | September 4, 2024

On September 1, 2024, Teamsters Local 633 held its official nomination meeting, where members gathered to select candidates for the upcoming union election. Despite the meeting being scheduled on Labor Day weekend, the event at Local 633 headquarters drew a solid turnout from the membership, all eager to participate in this critical democratic process.

As members settled into their seats, a striking divide became evident in the hall. On one side, supporters of the Members First Slate, clad in unified black t-shirts emblazoned with their logo, sat together in a show of solidarity. Across the room, supporters of the Power Slate, distinguished by their blue and gold shirts, occupied the opposite side. The meeting commenced with an official welcome from Jeffrey Padellaro, Secretary-Treasurer of Local 633. He addressed the members with a focus on the significance of the upcoming election, emphasizing its impact on the union's future direction. Following Padellaro's remarks, Scott Gove, Recording Secretary, took the floor to carefully read the bylaws related to the election process, ensuring that all members were fully informed of the nomination rules and setting a tone of transparency and fairness for the proceedings.

Nominations were then opened, overseen by a member from the International Brotherhood of Teamsters. Candidates were each nominated and the nominations seconded by members in good standing. Each candidate then accepted their nomination and had the opportunity to briefly address the members, outlining their commitment to Local 633. Many candidates expressed their dedication to strengthening the union, improving working conditions, and enhancing member services.

During the nominations, a surprising change occurred regarding the Power Slate's candidate for vice president. Those who had been following the Power Slate over the last several months expected Bob Browne's name to be called for the VP nomination. However, instead of Browne, Bob Stanley was nominated for vice president. Stanley had originally been running for a Trustee position, and in his place, Nathan LaFleur was nominated for Trustee. To their credit, the Power Slate later acknowledged on their Facebook page that Browne was ineligible for office due to an untimely dues payment while he was out on injury, demonstrating a commitment to transparency even in the face of setbacks.

Throughout the meeting, the Members First Slate, the incumbents, exuded a sense of unity and strength, speaking with confidence and authority as they accepted their nominations. Their cohesive front was evident as they sat together throughout the meeting, reinforcing their solidarity. In contrast, the Power Slate candidates, whose speeches were sometimes difficult to hear, were scattered throughout the room, blending in with the general membership. While this may have appeared less unified, it could strategically convey that they are not above the members but rather of the members, for the members.

The nominated candidates will now enter the campaign phase, where they will engage with members and share their platforms. The Power Slate began their campaign efforts back in July, but they have faced some resistance, particularly from the incumbents within the union hall. According to their website, the Power Slate alleges they encountered hostility from certain employers during visits, were instructed by a manager—who was directed by a business agent of the local—that they could not be on specific properties, and even met resistance in person at another site, where a significant number of the current leadership were present.

In response to the Power Slate's claims, the Members First Slate issued a letter on their Facebook page, denouncing the Power Slate and alleging they have been falsely presenting themselves as representatives of Local 633. The letter went further, describing the Power Slate members as "malcontents" with a "greedy agenda" and accused them of spreading false information. The Members First Slate criticized the Power Slate for lacking diversity, failing to negotiate any contracts, and for their overall inexperience, questioning their ability to represent the union effectively.

As the campaign intensifies, ballots should be mailed out on or around September 23, 2024. Ballots must be received by 8:00 a.m. on October 17, 2024 to be counted. Members who do not receive a ballot by October 1, 2024 should contact the Local 633 Union Hall. All members strongly encouraged to cast their votes and make their voices heard.

This election is a pivotal moment for Local 633, as the outcome will determine the leadership team that will guide the union through the next three-year term. The Members First Slate is campaigning on a platform of experience and substantial membership growth, emphasizing their track record and continuity. In contrast, the 633 Teamsters Power Slate is running on a platform of strong advocacy, transparency, and proactive representation, aiming to invigorate the union with a fresh approach. Members are urged to stay informed, participate in upcoming events, and exercise their right to vote in this crucial election.
